Delhi

1. Red Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Red Fort is an iconic symbol of Delhi. It showcases Mughal architecture and is a historical marvel.

2. India Gate

A war memorial, India Gate honors the soldiers who died in World War I. It's a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.

3. Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world, standing in the Qutub Complex. It's another UNESCO World Heritage Site.

4. Humayun's Tomb

This beautiful garden tomb is the final resting place of the Mughal Emperor Humayun, representing Persian architecture.

5. Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple, with its distinctive lotus flower shape, is a Bahá'í House of Worship. It's known for its peaceful ambiance.

Mumbai

1. Gateway of India

A prominent landmark, the Gateway of India is an arch monument built during the 20th century in Bombay. It overlooks the Arabian Sea.

2. Marine Drive

A scenic boulevard along the Arabian Sea, Marine Drive is known as the Queen's Necklace due to its shape at night.

3. Elephanta Caves

Located on Elephanta Island, these ancient caves house rock-cut sculptures and art dedicated to Lord Shiva.

4. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus

Formerly known as Victoria Terminus, this historic railway station is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an architectural gem.

5. Juhu Beach

One of the most famous beaches in Mumbai, Juhu Beach offers a relaxing escape and is a popular spot for locals.

Kolkata

1. Victoria Memorial

A large marble building, Victoria Memorial is a dedication to Queen Victoria. It houses a museum and is surrounded by gardens.

2. Howrah Bridge

An iconic cantilever bridge, Howrah Bridge is a symbol of Kolkata. It connects Howrah and Kolkata over the Hooghly River.

3. Dakshineswar Kali Temple

Dedicated to Goddess Kali, this temple is known for its serene surroundings and the Navaratna style of architecture

4. Indian Museum

Established in 1814, the Indian Museum is one of the oldest in Asia and showcases a vast collection of artifacts.

5. Science City

A dynamic science center, Science City features interactive exhibits, a space theater, and a butterfly garden.

Chennai

1. Marina Beach

One of the longest beaches in the world, Marina Beach is a popular destination for locals and tourists to relax and unwind.

2. Kapaleeshwarar Temple

This ancient Hindu temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is known for its Dravidian architecture.

3. Fort St. George

Built by the British East India Company, Fort St. George is the first English fortress in India. It now houses the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly.

4. San Thome Basilica

A Roman Catholic basilica, San Thome Basilica is built over the tomb of St. Thomas, an apostle of Jesus.

5. Government Museum

Established in 1851, the Government Museum in Chennai is one of the oldest in India, featuring a diverse collection of artifacts.

Jaipur

1. Hawa Mahal

Known as the Palace of Winds, Hawa Mahal is a stunning structure with a unique honeycomb design, allowing royal ladies to observe street festivals without being seen.

2. City Palace

A beautiful palace complex, City Palace showcases a blend of Rajput, Mughal, and European architectural styles.

3. Amber Fort

Perched on a hill, Amber Fort is a majestic fortress with intricate architecture and panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

4. Jantar Mantar

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Jantar Mantar is an astronomical observatory with large-scale instruments used for accurate timekeeping.

5. Jal Mahal

Situated in the middle of Man Sagar Lake, Jal Mahal is a water palace with a picturesque setting, offering boat rides for visitors.
